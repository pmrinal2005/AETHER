# A.I.M. — Research Reference Text (Phase 0.4)

> These excerpts are stored for **verbatim use in UI copy and pitch materials**.
> They frame WHY cross-protocol Identity + Reputation + Moderation is an unsolved
> problem that A.I.M. addresses as neutral infrastructure.

---

## FIDO Alliance (April 2026 announcement) — Authentication solved, Identity/Reputation not

> "The industry has converged on strong authentication and payment authorization
> rails for AI agents, but **cross-protocol agent identity, portable reputation,
> and post-authorization revocation remain open problems** outside the scope of
> the payments and authentication coalition."

**A.I.M. positioning:** Payments/auth are coalition-owned (Visa/Mastercard/Google/FIDO).
A.I.M. sits *above* those rails as a neutral trust bureau — it does not compete with
payment settlement.

---

## Cyber Security Agency of Singapore (CSA) — Oct 2025 Guidelines Addendum

> "Organizations should maintain **human-in-the-loop oversight** for autonomous
> agent actions, with **workflow mapping** that records each agent's delegated
> scope, and the ability to intervene, throttle, or revoke authorization when an
> agent deviates from its mapped workflow."

**A.I.M. positioning:** The retro Buddy List *is* the human-in-the-loop oversight
surface; the Delegation Graph *is* the workflow map; the Revocation Bus *is* the
intervene/throttle/revoke capability.

---

## OWASP Non-Human Identity (NHI) Top 10 — 2025

> **NHI4: Improper Offboarding** — "Failure to promptly and completely deauthorize
> non-human identities (service accounts, agents, API keys) when they are
> decommissioned, compromised, or their task completes, leaving standing privileges
> that can be abused."

**A.I.M. positioning:** The Real-Time Revocation Bus + "Buddy Signed Off" animation
directly demonstrate immediate, complete offboarding.

---

## RepuNet (May 2025) — Dual-level reputation via repeated public-goods game

> "Agents accumulate reputation through **direct interaction feedback** and
> **indirect gossip propagation** across a network. New agents overcome the
> cold-start problem by participating in **repeated public-goods-game rounds**
> against reference peers, where cooperation is rewarded and defection is
> penalized, yielding a bootstrap reputation prior to full network participation."

**A.I.M. positioning:** Buddy Bootcamp™ = the public-goods-game gauntlet;
AgentScore's dual-level (direct + gossip) model = RepuNet's mechanism.
